PostgreSQL Backup and Point-in-Time Recovery

Production PostgreSQL clusters use continuous archiving with WAL shipping
to object storage (S3) for point-in-time recovery (PITR). Base backups are
taken nightly with pg_basebackup and retained for 30 days; WAL segments
are archived every 60 seconds, giving a recovery point objective (RPO) of
under one minute.

To restore to a specific moment:
  1. Provision a new cluster from the most recent base backup taken before
     the target time.
  2. Configure recovery.conf with restore_command pointing at the WAL
     archive and recovery_target_time set to the desired timestamp.
  3. Start PostgreSQL; it will replay WAL until the target, then promote.
  4. Verify by querying a known checkpoint table; rotate the cluster's
     replication slot identifier.

Verification: a recovery drill is performed monthly against a non-prod
copy. The drill measures recovery time objective (RTO) — currently 22
minutes for a 500 GB cluster — and validates checksums against a known
golden snapshot. Failed drills page the on-call DBA.

Logical backups (pg_dump) are NOT used for disaster recovery; they are
reserved for schema migrations and table-level extraction.
