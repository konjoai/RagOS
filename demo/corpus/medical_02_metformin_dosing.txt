Metformin Dosing — Type 2 Diabetes Initiation and Titration

Metformin is the preferred first-line oral antihyperglycemic agent for adult
patients with type 2 diabetes mellitus, absent contraindications. Initiate
at 500 mg orally once or twice daily with meals to reduce gastrointestinal
side effects (nausea, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort). Titrate by 500 mg
weekly as tolerated, to a maximum of 2000 mg per day in divided doses.

Renal function must be assessed before initiation and at least annually.
Estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) thresholds:
  - eGFR ≥ 60 mL/min/1.73 m²: standard dosing.
  - eGFR 45–59: continue with caution; monitor renal function every 3–6 months.
  - eGFR 30–44: do not initiate; reduce dose by 50% if already on therapy.
  - eGFR < 30: contraindicated. Discontinue.

Lactic acidosis is a rare but serious complication. Hold metformin during
acute illness, dehydration, or before iodinated contrast imaging in
patients with eGFR < 60. Vitamin B12 levels should be monitored every 1–2
years on long-term therapy due to risk of malabsorption.
